Underwater Mcdonald's Dream

This was quite an interesting dream. Although its rather short, it was funny enough to record. To set the scene, I found myself eating with some friends in a Mcdonald’s that was halfway underwater in the middle of the ocean. Although there was no water flooding inside, I could see the water level reaching about halfway up the door outside the restaurant. On the outside the area was beautiful. The restaurant was surrounded by a light blue/ teal water you would see on remote islands with shallow waters. In the distance, I could see a nearby Island maybe about a mile off the coast. Somehow this must have been a feature of a resort that I had been visiting, although I had no recollection of how I actually got there. However, the inside of the restaurant was a different story. There were puddles of water on the ground everywhere and multiple wet floor signs out. The staff seemed ghetto and I was afraid of the food quality. I remember there was a Mexican worker going around with a trashcan asking people if they had any trash they needed thrown away. I told her that I had some and she looked right above me and completely ignored me and kept moving. I remember thinking that it was the most ghetto Mcdonald’s I had ever been too. Yet it was surrounded by such a nice area. All of a sudden the black woman manager started running toward a window on the floor beside a table. There were three black women trying to swim up to the window and break in to rob it or something. She went outside through the window and began to fight them underwater and ward them off, it seemed like nothing out of the ordinary here. They were all swimming around fighting when someone told me to go lock the door quickly. I got up and noticed one of the women was breaking in the front. I tried to lock the door but I realized that the door had already been locked, and I had unlocked it by accident. She started pushing the door in water began to pour in the restaurant. I used all my might and I was barely able to close it. That’s where the dream began to fade.

The significance? Although it was a dream, I can say I am probably one of the only people in history, if not only person, to experience what it would be like to eat at Mcdonald’s in the middle of the ocean halfway under water.
